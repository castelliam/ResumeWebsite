How It All Started

I was not always the eager student I am today. When I started programming, I was a Junior in high school. I decided to stop taking french, which meant I had to sign up for a different elective. Thinking Computer Science would be dealing with Word and Excel, I thought, "Yeah, I'll be good at that." Was I wrong or what?

For the entire year, I struggled every single day. I had a friend that kept me going and practicing. I also has a relentless teacher that I will never forget. He forced me to see him every morning before class and while it did help, I slinked into the room each morning, dreading the time I was in there. By the end of that year, I had rasied my grade, made a leap frogger game in Java, and was ready to go ahead and struggle through the next class.

Since then, programming has been the only subject in school that I've wanted to work hard for, even when I was struggling. Now, in CODEcamp, I'm learning more than I could've hoped and loving every minute of it. People laugh when I joke around with other members around the Corridor about how great it would be if we had CODEcamp lockins and reward a pin every time a student finishes a class. But, in all serious, it has made me even more passionate and excited about start into a career pursuing Computer Science.

I am in for a lifetime of all the challenges and excitement that this field has to offer.
